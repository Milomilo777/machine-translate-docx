════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP A — RE-ALIGN  (RUN FIRST — MANDATORY)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

BACKGROUND:
Stage 1 translation often produces "carry-over" output: the translator
treated 2–7 consecutive EN lines as one paragraph and spread the Persian
translation as raw fragments across those lines, ignoring EN line boundaries.
Step A detects and corrects this before any merge decision.

─── A1. DETECT CARRY-OVER GROUPS ──────────────────────
A carry-over group = a run of consecutive EN lines whose FA values,
when concatenated in order, form a coherent Persian passage,
AND whose EN lines together form one or more complete sentences.

Signal patterns (any one is enough to flag a group):
  • FA for line N ends MID-WORD or without punctuation, AND
    FA for line N+1 starts with a lowercase continuation word.
  • FA for line N is clearly semantically incomplete without line N+1's FA.
  • One or more lines in the run have an EMPTY FA ("") even though
    the EN line has real content.

─── A2. REASSEMBLE & RE-SPLIT ──────────────────────────
For each detected carry-over group of N consecutive lines:

  Step A2a — CONCATENATE:
    FA_COMBINED = join all FA values in the group (left-to-right, space-separated).

  Step A2b — RE-SPLIT into exactly N chunks:
    Split FA_COMBINED at natural Persian phrase/sentence boundaries
    (strong: . ! ?  |  medium: ،  |  weak: semantic clause boundary)
    such that each chunk semantically corresponds to its paired EN line.
    Aim: chunk K should be the best Persian equivalent of EN line K.

  Step A2c — ASSIGN:
    Assign chunk K → key L_K.
    If the reassembled FA has fewer natural splits than N lines,
    assign the remaining lines "" (FALLBACK_EMPTY) — Step B will DOUBLE them.

For lines NOT in any carry-over group: assign FA as-is (KEPT_INTACT).

─── A3. SPLIT CODES (for _split_reasoning) ────────────
  "SPLIT_SENTENCE"  — chunk ends with sentence-final punctuation (. ! ?)
  "SPLIT_NATURAL"   — chunk ends with ، or at a semantic clause boundary
  "SPLIT_SPECIAL"   — header, label, URL, or production note line
  "FALLBACK_EMPTY"  — EN line has content but FA assigned "" after reassembly
  "KEPT_INTACT"     — line was NOT in a carry-over group; FA value used as-is

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP B — DOUBLE / MERGE  (RUN ON STEP A OUTPUT ONLY)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

For each numerically consecutive pair (L_N, L_N+1) — process left-to-right:

─── B1. VETO CHECKS (ordered — first match wins → KEEP_SEPARATE) ───
  V1. ACTION  : Either EN line is a stage direction, production note,
                URL, or any text enclosed in [ ] or ( ).
  V2. SPEAKER : Either EN line starts with a name/label followed by colon
                (e.g. "VO:", "HOST:", "Caregiver:", "SM:").
  V3. HEADER  : Either EN line is ALL-CAPS or ends with a bare colon :.
  V4. LENGTH  : len(FA_N + " " + FA_N+1) > 54 characters.
  V5. GAP     : Line numbers are not consecutive (differ by more than 1).
  V6. LOCK    : Either line was already used in a DOUBLE_MERGED pair.
  V7. EMPTY   : Both FA values are empty strings.

─── B2. MERGE CONDITIONS (pass ALL vetoes + at least ONE) ──────────
  M1. BRIDGE  : FA_N ends with ، OR FA_N+1 starts with:
                و / اما / که / پس / زیرا / چون / یا / با / تا
  M2. SEMANTIC: EN pair forms an inseparable grammatical unit:
                preposition + object ("of isolation" / "in the park"),
                adjective + noun, verb + complement, fragment + completion.
  M3. SPIRITUAL: Both EN lines are part of a blessing, prayer, or wish.
  M4. EMPTY_B : FA_N+1 is "" (FALLBACK_EMPTY from Step A) — copy FA_N to both.

─── B3. EXECUTION ──────────────────────────────────────
  DOUBLE_MERGED:
    combined = FA_N + " " + FA_N+1   (or just FA_N if FA_N+1 is "")
    Assign combined → BOTH L_N and L_N+1.
    Mark both as LOCKED (cannot participate in another merge pair).

  KEEP_SEPARATE:
    L_N keeps its Step A value.
    L_N+1 is evaluated again as the start of the next pair.

  ODD REMAINING LINE (no pair partner):
    Keep as-is → code "KEPT_SINGLE".

─── B4. MERGE CODES (for _merge_reasoning) ─────────────
  "MERGED_BRIDGE"    — merged via M1 bridge word/punctuation
  "MERGED_SEMANTIC"  — merged via M2 grammatical unit
  "MERGED_SPIRITUAL" — merged via M3 blessing/prayer
  "MERGED_EMPTY"     — merged via M4 empty second line
  "KEPT_ACTION"      — blocked by V1
  "KEPT_SPEAKER"     — blocked by V2
  "KEPT_HEADER"      — blocked by V3
  "KEPT_LENGTH"      — blocked by V4
  "KEPT_GAP"         — blocked by V5
  "KEPT_LOCK"        — blocked by V6
  "KEPT_NO_BRIDGE"   — passed vetoes but no merge condition met
  "KEPT_SINGLE"      — odd line with no consecutive partner
  "KEPT_INTACT"      — single-line, no carry-over, no pair

► Example 1 — Carry-over 3 lines → RE-ALIGN + DOUBLE_MERGED + KEEP_SEPARATE

INPUT:
{

    "L56": "I cleared the ice",
    "L57": "and gave her food,",
    "L58": "but she quickly led me outside",
    "L59": "to her snow-covered nest."
  },
  "target_texts": {
    "L56": "یخ‌ها را کنار زدم و به او غذا",
    "L57": "دادم، اما او خیلی زود",
    "L58": "مرا به بیرون برد؛",
    "L59": "به لانهٔ پوشیده از برفش."
  }
}

STEP A reasoning:
  L56 FA ends mid-phrase ("غذا" — no punctuation) → carry-over detected
  L57 FA starts with continuation ("دادم") → confirmed group L56-L57-L58-L59
  FA_COMBINED = "یخ‌ها را کنار زدم و به او غذا دادم، اما خیلی زود مرا به بیرون برد؛ به لانهٔ پوشیده از برفش."

    L56 → "یخ‌ها را کنار زدم و به او غذا دادم،"   (matches "I cleared the ice and gave food")

    L59 → "به لانهٔ پوشیده از برفش."               (matches "to her snow-covered nest")

STEP B reasoning:
  L56_L57: FA_N="یخ‌ها را کنار زدم و به او غذا دادم،", FA_N+1="" → M4 EMPTY_B → MERGED_EMPTY
  L58_L59: FA_N ends "؛", FA_N+1 ends "." — no bridge, no semantic unit → KEPT_NO_BRIDGE

OUTPUT:
{
  "_split_reasoning": {
    "L56": "SPLIT_NATURAL",

EDGE CASES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

• EMPTY input line (L_N has EN="" and FA=""):
  → Assign "" to output. SPLIT_SPECIAL. Skip in Step B.

• URL lines (EN is a URL):
  → Assign "" to output. SPLIT_SPECIAL. Skip in Step B.

• ALL-CAPS lines (HEADERS like "HOST INTRO:", "1ST ANIMAL-PEOPLE VIDEO:"):
  → Assign "" to output. SPLIT_SPECIAL. V3 HEADER veto in Step B.

• 3-way carry-over where middle line should be DOUBLE:
  → Complete Step A first (all lines get proper FA chunks),
    THEN evaluate pairs in Step B left-to-right.
