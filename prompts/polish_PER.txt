<MANDATORY_NORMALIZATION>
Hygiene transforms. Apply on ALL lines in PHASE_0_BASE.
MN-1 QUOTE_STYLE — " " ONLY. «» → " ". Sentence-terminal punct OUTSIDE the closing quote. EXCEPTION: punctuation belonging to the quoted title/question/exclamation stays INSIDE ("What the Health?" → "سلامتی یعنی چه؟"). Nested → ASCII '. No new quotes added.
       NO_QUOTE list — NEVER wrap in " ": job titles & honorifics (President, دکتر، استاد), globally famous entities (Apple, Paris, NASA), names immediately after a title anchor.
MN-2 EZAFE_FORM — silent ه → هٔ (خانهٔ، درجهٔ، اتحادیهٔ). Never ه‌ی. ZWNJ for prefixes (می‌، نمی‌)، suffixes (ها، تر، ترین)، established compounds (هم‌خانه، هم‌ساکن، دست‌وپاگیر، به‌شمار، بی‌خانمان). Avoid decorative or post-preposition ZWNJ.
MN-3 HARAKAT — strip all by default. Exceptions:
       1) Established VERBATIM/LITERARY/scriptural forms where attested harakat is essential — fully preserved; NOT counted in ceiling.
       2) Rare unresolved homographs — both required: genuine homograph AND unresolvable by ±5 context. Ceiling/100: NEWS/FACILITATOR ≤3, SAGE ≤5.
MN-4 NO_BAASHE — "باشه"/"باشهٔ" → "بله" / "درست است" / "حتماً". Applies inside ordinary quotes too.
       COLLOQUIAL_NORMALIZE (broadcast-safe): آره→بله | میشه→می‌شود | میرم→می‌روم | خوبه→خوب است. Preserve communicative function.
       FILLERS (persona-aware): Yeah→بله | OK→بسیار خب/خوب | Sure→حتماً | Of course→البته | Thanks→سپاس/متشکرم/ممنون | Sorry→ببخشید/متأسفم.
MN-5 NO_TOOSAT — "توسط" treatment:
       AGENT_KNOWN definition (operational): the agent is "known" when [EN]
       names the actor that is grammatical subject of the active form
       ("by Congress" → Congress is the agent). The agent is "unknown/absent"
       when [EN] is agentless passive without a "by X" phrase
       ("the law was passed" with no agent named) OR when [EN] explicitly
       hides the agent for rhetorical or institutional reasons.

       PREDICATE line, agent KNOWN → active voice within same line.
         [EN] "the law was passed by Congress" → کنگره قانون را تصویب کرد
       PREDICATE line, agent UNKNOWN/ABSENT → preserve Persian passive; replace
       "توسط" with a clean Persian passive ("قانون تصویب شد") — Persian
       agentless passive is correct and conventional in news/legal register.
         [EN] "the law was passed" → قانون تصویب شد (no agent invented)
       FRAGMENT line → no active conversion; replace "توسط" with prepositional
       (از سوی / به دست / زیر نظر / با حمایت) by context — same agent-known
       rule applies (preserve only if agent is in source).
       👑 MASTER line — prefer MINIMAL prepositional repair over active recast
       when active voice would alter rhetorical emphasis.

       Reflective test before recast: "Does [EN] name the actor that would
       become the active-form subject? If no, the source chose agentlessness
       deliberately — preserve it." Never invent agent.
MN-6 PUNCT_NORMALIZE — outside protected quotes (LS-10) AND outside number contexts:
       "؛" and ASCII ";" → "،" or clause split within SAME line.
       ASCII "?" → "؟". ASCII "," → "،".
       NUMBER parsing precedes this rule: ASCII comma between digits handled by MN-10, NEVER converted to "،".
       No Oxford comma in LISTS. Comma before "و" IS PERMITTED for independent clauses or to prevent misreading.
       No new brackets, glosses, translator notes added by polisher.
       DASH_NORMALIZE: any — / – / -- variant → spaced ASCII " - ". Never en/em dash.
       Do not glue sentence-terminal punctuation to ALLOWED_LATIN spans.
MN-7 SERIAL — قسمت N از M. قسمت locked.
MN-8 LATIN_LEAKAGE — outside ALLOWED_LATIN → Persianise (AI → هوش مصنوعی | EU → اتحادیهٔ اروپا | DNA → دی‌ان‌ای). Applies inside ordinary quotes.
       LATIN_PHRASES (not TECH_LOCK): per capita → سرانه | de facto → عملاً | status quo → وضع موجود | et al. → و همکاران.
MN-9 ORTHOGRAPHY — Arabic ك → Persian ک. Arabic ي → Persian ی. Remove tatweel (ـ). Use only U+200C for ZWNJ. Does NOT apply inside ALLOWED_LATIN or LS-10 verbatim quotes.
MN-10 NUMBER_FORMAT — apply NUMBERS policy (digit Persianisation, thousands separator "٬", quantity-only rounding, currency/ordinal/fraction). Value-locked.
       Does NOT alter numbers inside LS-10 verbatim quotes or ALLOWED_LATIN spans.
       WRITTEN_NUMBERS — factual written-out numbers → Persian digits when unambiguous (ninety percent → ۹۰٪ | ten people → ۱۰ نفر). Skip for idioms/scripture/titles/Master rhetoric.
       NEVER apply rounding to: years (2026), IDs/codes (F-16, S01E05, H5N1), episode/article/legal numbers, addresses, postcodes, timestamps, model/serial/phone numbers.
       Apply MILLION rule BEFORE THOUSAND. ۳٬۰۰۰→۳ هزار | ۱۵٬۰۰۰→۱۵ هزار | ۱٬۰۰۰→هزار | ۲٬۰۰۰٬۰۰۰→۲ میلیون. Non-round (۱٬۵۰۰/۳٬۷۵۰) stay.
       Date: D Month YYYY / Month D, YYYY → ۴ مارس ۲۰۲۶. Ambiguous numeric (03/04/2026) → Persianize digits, preserve order/separator.
       Month names (locked): January→ژانویه | February→فوریه | March→مارس | April→آوریل | May→مه | June→ژوئن | July→ژوئیه | August→اوت | September→سپتامبر | October→اکتبر | November→نوامبر | December→دسامبر.
       Currency: $5 million → ۵ میلیون دلار. Ordinals: 1st → نخست/اول. Fractions: 1/2 → نیم / یک‌دوم.
</MANDATORY_NORMALIZATION>

<SEMANTIC_ANCHORS>
Run on EVERY line in PHASE_0_BASE (exception to EN_ROLE default). NOT retranslation; anchor mismatch only.
SA-1 NEGATION — affirmative ↔ negative match.
       SCOPE_ATTACHMENT_GUARD: do not let a local attachment turn a clause-level negation/action into a noun-level concept. e.g. "not eating" → "نمی‌خورد" (clause-level), NOT "نه-خوردن"/"روزه‌داری" (noun-level error) unless source clearly intends that nominalization.
SA-2 NUMBERS — digit value, quantity, percent, range match.
SA-3 NAMES — proper nouns transliterated correctly.
SA-4 DATES — D Month YYYY match.
SA-5 SPEAKER + COREFERENCE — who/whom match. Pronoun and ellipsis chains preserved across line context. If Persian grammar forces an explicit referent, choose the least-committal context-consistent form and keep it consistent.
SA-6 MODALITY — distinguish:
       obligation (must/shall) → باید | prohibition (must not) → نباید
       permission (may/can — license) → مجاز است / می‌تواند | ability (can) → می‌تواند
       possibility (may/might/could) → ممکن است / شاید
       logical inference (must be) → حتماً / لابد | recommendation (should) → باید / بهتر است
       counterfactuality (could have / would have) → می‌توانست / ممکن بود
     Do NOT map all may/can/must to one Persian form.
SA-7 CORE_PREDICATE — main verb action match (different action class only). IDIOM-AWARE: if Persian idiom preserves communicative meaning of an EN idiom/metaphor, surface verb mismatch is NOT an anchor error (made my day → دلم را شاد کرد is NOT a SA-7 failure).
SA-8 SPIRITUAL_TERM — sky/heaven/heavenly mapping (آسمان physical / بهشت divine / بهشتی spiritual quality).
SA-9 INTENSITY — source-supported intensifiers preserved; translator-invented certainty/intensity removed/scaled.
SA-10 ASPECT/TIME — already / still / no longer / yet / began / continued / used to / has been match.
SA-11 PROTECTED_SPAN_PRESENCE — ALLOWED_LATIN spans (W1–W4 + ACRONYM-IN-PARENS) present in [EN] must appear byte-id in output, unless line is corrupt. Overridden by BLANK POLICY when [FA] is blank.
SA-12 LOGICAL_CONNECTOR — because / although / despite / unless / so that / in order to / therefore / however: logical relation preserved.
SA-13 COMPARISON_SCOPE — only / even / at least / at most / more than / less than / no more than / not all / all: quantifier scope preserved.
SA-14 ONTOLOGICAL_REPAIR — three patterns:
       (a) Inanimate subject + human/religious action ("اعتکاف یخچال" for "glacier retreats") → reverse-engineer EN and use contextual Persian synonym ("عقب‌نشینی یخچال").
       (b) Possessive genitive on an entity that cannot physically/biologically possess the noun. Reverse-engineer from [EN] ±5 lines to identify the correct referent.
             "گره‌های غاز" (EN ±5 lines say "tangled in a rope … untangle her") → "گره‌های طناب"
             "بال‌های اتومبیل" (literal car, not metaphor) → "بال‌های هواپیما"
             "پنجه‌های انسان" (when EN "claws" was metaphor) → "ناخن‌های ..."
       (c) Body-part adjective on entity lacking that part — same reverse-engineering rule.
       METAPHOR_GUARD: if [EN] itself uses an obvious figurative construction (e.g. "wings of imagination", "claws of fate"), the [FA] mirror is intentional poetic mapping — DO NOT repair. Only fire when the [EN] is literal and the [FA] possessive is biologically/physically impossible.
       NEVER applies to SMTV ontology terms (LS-2, LS-4, LS-8).

SA-15 INVENTION_GUARD — every content noun, adjective, and modifier in [FA] must trace back to a referent or clear implication in [EN]. If [FA] adds material with no source, remove the unjustified word/phrase.
       EXAMPLES:
         [EN] with the friends                → [FA] "با گروه مذهبیِ دوستانی"  → fix: "با دوستانی"  («مذهبی» invented)
         [EN] the children played              → [FA] "کودکان عاقل بازی کردند"  → fix: "کودکان بازی کردند" («عاقل» invented)
         [EN] he ate dinner                    → [FA] "او شام مفصلی خورد"      → fix: "او شام خورد" («مفصل» invented)
       EXCEPTIONS — do NOT treat as invented:
         (1) Function words Persian grammar requires: relative «که»، linking «و»، object-marker «را»، ezafe particle.
         (2) Persian-explicit subject pronouns where English drops them
             ("ate dinner" → "[او] شام خورد" — pronoun OK).
         (3) Honorific/register adjustments mandated by LS-4 SPIRITUAL_TITLES,
             LS-7 SPEECH_VERBS, or LS-11 BRAND_CHANNEL (the canonical SMTV
             rendering may add words; that's the lock, not invention).
         (4) BRACKETGLOSS content already present in [EN] brackets.
       REFLECTIVE TEST before removal: "Does every noun/adjective in [FA] have a clear source word OR a clear contextual implication in [EN]?" If no for any single token AND no exception applies → drop the unjustified material.
       BOUNDS: SA-15 is removal-only. NEVER add words to [FA] that [EN] has but the translator dropped — that case is a [FA]-incomplete translation problem, not polish scope.

SA-12 / SA-13 ANCHOR-ONLY constraint: if the logical/scope relation is already unambiguous in [FA], do NOT insert an explicit connector merely to mirror [EN]. These anchors detect mismatch, not literal token mapping.

On MISMATCH:
  [FA] anchor wrong → fix the anchor only, within same line, within G7.
  Anchor matches but phrasing differs → leave it (HANDOFF_TRUST).
</SEMANTIC_ANCHORS>

<MONOLINGUAL_LENS>
Two diagnostic probes, run silently on EVERY line AFTER PHASE_0_BASE and BEFORE SCAN. Their job is to set the threshold for ⬜ CLEAN: a line that passes hygiene + anchors but fails either probe is NOT CLEAN — it is 🟠 MT_RESIDUE and must enter EDIT.

THE LENS: imagine the reader is a literate Persian monolingual who has never seen the English source, never seen any English at all. They read this one Persian line alone. The probes ask what THAT reader's ear and eye would catch.

M1. COLLOCATION_PROBE — Persian's collocational web.
    For every salient pairing inside the line, ask:
      • (noun + adjective): does Persian writing actually pair them?
      • (subject + verb): does this subject normally carry this verb in Persian?
      • (modifier + head): is the modifier from the head's semantic domain?
    Calque tell-tales (recall-only, not a closed list):
      — an inanimate or temporal noun cast as an emotive/perceptual subject
        ("era saw advances", "the algorithm decided", "the mission strengthened it")
      — a body organ taking a mood adjective ("a happy gut", "a sad liver")
      — a tool/utility adjective grafted onto a non-tool ("a versatile dish")
      — an English-shaped chain of nominalisations the Persian reader would
        unpack into a verbal clause ("provision of services took place")
      — a wonder-register lexeme dropped into a clinical or factual context
        ("a magical effect on blood sugar", "a treasure of resistant starch")
      — IDIOM CALQUES (2026-05-17): an EN idiom translated word-by-word
        whose Persian surface is grammatically fluent but no Persian
        reader has seen it in Persian prose. Diagnostic examples (do
        NOT pattern-match; retrieve the SHAPE from your Persian corpus):
          "در پوست خود" (in his own skin) — body-locative figure not native to FA
          "از بشقامش کنار رفت" (got off my plate) — locative-removal figure not native
          "معده‌ام می‌پذیرفت" (could stomach) — body-verb pairing not native
          "فضای مهمانی" (party scene) — abstract-space noun not used this way in FA
          "فضایی برای رشد" (space to grow) — same abstract-space pattern
          "معیار سرگرمی" (baseline for fun) — quantification-noun not paired with «سرگرمی» in FA
          "حیوانی که زمانی بود" (the animal it once was) — temporal-ellipsis structure not native
        If your DRAFT contains one of these AND the swap to a native
        Persian rendering preserves meaning + polarity + quantifier +
        modality, prefer the native form. Full reference list and the
        decision rules sit in EDIT ⑤c — this probe DETECTS, ⑤c
        CATALOGUES + ACTS. Both must succeed for ⬜ CLEAN.
    The probe SUCCEEDS (line stays CLEAN) when every pairing is one a Persian
    reader has actually seen in Persian prose, journalism, or scholarly writing
    on this kind of topic. The test is CORPUS-presence, not surface grammar:
    a grammatical pairing that no Persian text actually uses is STILL a
    calque. Sharpened reflective question: "Have I ever read THIS pairing in
    Persian outside of a translation? If only inside translations — it's
    translatorese." If even one pairing fails this check → 🟠 MT_RESIDUE.

M2. IDIOMATIC_AVAILABILITY_PROBE — Persian's stock of fixed expressions.
    Ask: does the line's core proposition have a Persian idiom, کنایه, or
    تعبیر رایج that conveys it more naturally, at the same register and
    fidelity? If yes — and the swap costs no meaning, no quantifier, no
    polarity — prefer the idiom.
    Recall-only examples to illustrate the SHAPE of the probe (do NOT
    pattern-match these specific pairs; retrieve from your own Persian
    corpus whatever fits THIS line):
      "from generation to generation" → "سینه به سینه" (when context is
        oral transmission of culture/memory, not literal descent)
      "let's dig in / let's eat" (table opener) → "بزن بریم" / "مشغول
        شویم" / "نوش جان"
      "carb crash" → "افت قند / افت انرژی پس از کربوهیدرات"
      "era saw advances" → "در عصر X پیشرفت‌های ... رخ داد"
    The probe SUCCEEDS (no swap) when no fixed Persian expression matches
    the line's proposition cleanly, OR the existing wording already IS the
    idiomatic form. Otherwise: 🟠 MT_RESIDUE with a candidate substitution
    in mind for EDIT step ③/④/⑤.

GATING:
  These probes RAISE detection sensitivity; they do NOT bypass G-gates.
  After a probe fires, the candidate edit STILL passes through:
    G1 MEANING (≥5% shift → ABANDON), G2 DOUBT, G7 MEANING_PRESERVATION,
    G8 BOUNDARY, G9 HANDOFF_TRUST.
  Both probes are advisory, not lexical bans — no specific word or pattern
  is forbidden by these probes alone. They train you to ASK the question,
  then trust your Persian-corpus judgement to answer it line by line.
  Triggering both probes on the SAME pairing is the strongest signal that
  EDIT must produce a non-trivial alternative; triggering neither is the
  strongest signal that ⬜ CLEAN is the honest call.
